Social Debugger / educational simulator

Social Debugger

Will your town survive?

▶ https://larai-w.github.io/social-system-debugger/

For civics, CS and inquiry classes / No prep, browser only, free, no sign-up

01 — What this is

Experience how a society breaks, with your own hands

Move the parameters (sliders) and watch a society and its regional infrastructure come apart in front of you.

This is not a game and not a prophecy. It is a tool for thinking about why things break.

Not a machine for guessing the right answer, but a bench for trying "what if..." by changing the conditions.

02 — The four themes

One app, four ways to break

PAGE 1
Information
Filter bubbles and overfitting: information bias quietly erodes diversity and trust.
PAGE 2
Infrastructure
Shrinking population, efficiency drives, successor shortage — efficient yet disaster-fragile.
PAGE 3
Cognition
The mechanics of unstoppable outrage, and the way back from it.
PAGE 4
Stakeholder
asymmetry
The channel dynamics by which affected voices are drowned out by spectators.

In class, pick one of these as today's theme.

03 — 3-minute demo (teacher projects)

Show the collapse, change one thing, save it

  1. Open PAGE 1 and tap the preset "⚡ Weimar Collapse".
  2. The society starts running — let the class watch it collapse.
  3. Rewind to the past, change just one slider, and see if it recovers.

Ask: "What single change would have let this town survive?"

04 — What to notice

If they catch this, the lesson worked

  • Collapse comes not from one cause, but when several conditions overlap.
  • And yet one person, one move can change the future — if it isn't too late.

Students learn to ask not "who is to blame" but "which conditions, together, make things fragile".

05 — Discussion questions

Questions you can put on the board as-is

  • "When we only see what we like, why does the whole society become fragile?" (PAGE 1)
  • "When is optimization the right answer, and when is it fatal? (the value of redundancy)" (PAGE 2)
  • "Is runaway anger a weakness of will, or a structure?" (PAGE 3)
  • "If you could rewind one thing in a collapsed town, what would you protect?" (inquiry)

06 — A 50-minute format

How to run one period

10 min
Individual play on one theme.
10 min
Form a hypothesis for a "surviving configuration".
15 min
Test it by experimenting with the parameters.
15 min
Groups share: which intervention worked, and what it maps to in the real world.

A 10-minute opener also works (project a preset → collapse → one question in pairs).

07 — Handling notes (important)

A tool for understanding, not for blame

This app is a fictional simulation. Do not map it onto real countries, parties, or people.
In class, treat it as "a story about structures that can happen to any society" — not about anyone or anywhere in particular.
Using its results to judge a real region, person, or party goes beyond what this model can support.
