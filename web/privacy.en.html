Social Debugger — Privacy Policy

In short: no account, no personal data collected. Your records stay only on your own device.

1. Information we do not collect

No account is required to use this app. We do not collect personal information such as your name, email address, or phone number.

  • The only exception: if you choose to enter your email address when contacting us through the in-app "💬 Feedback", we use that address only to reply to you, and for nothing else.

2. Information stored on your device

Your settings, town name, discovery log, and weekly-clear records are stored in your browser's localStorage (the native app also mirrors them into the app's Preferences), on your device only.

  • This data is never sent to our servers.
  • Clearing your browser's site data, or deleting the app, removes these records.

3. Analytics

We currently use no analytics. Nothing is measured — not even visit counts.

  • In the future we may enable anonymous, cookie-free aggregate analytics (a privacy-preserving tool such as Plausible that does not identify individuals). If we do, we will update this page.

4. Third-party services

  • We load a charting library (Chart.js) from a CDN (an external content delivery network). As a result, the CDN provider may keep the usual web access logs (such as your IP address), as with any web request.
  • Sharing to X or LINE happens only when you explicitly tap a share button, and is handled under each service's own terms. If you don't tap it, no information is passed to those services.

5. Notifications (native app only)

In the native app (iOS / Android), notifications are limited to a once-a-week local notification. It stays within your device; no information leaves the device.

  • You can revoke notification permission at any time from your OS settings.

6. Contact

For any privacy questions, please reach us through the in-app "💬 Feedback", or via the Issues on our GitHub repository (larai-w/social-system-debugger).

This app is free, non-commercial, and open source. We handle no data beyond what is described above. Its purpose is not to gather anyone's information, but to let you experience "structures that can happen to any society".
Effective date: July 9, 2026. If we change this policy, we will announce it on this page.
